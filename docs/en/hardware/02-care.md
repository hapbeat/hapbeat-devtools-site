This page covers best practices for keeping your Hapbeat in good condition over time.

## Charging and Battery Management

### How to Charge

- Charge using the included **USB Type-C data cable** (a charge-only cable can supply power, but we recommend keeping a data cable on hand for configuration changes)
- While charging, the **LED glows red**; it switches to **LED green** when fully charged
- Connect to a PC USB port or a USB charger rated at 5 V / 1 A or higher

### Estimated Battery Life

Battery life varies significantly depending on usage environment, vibration frequency, and whether the OLED display is on or off. See [Specifications & Certifications](./specifications/) for specific estimates.

### Tips to Extend Battery Life

- **Avoid leaving the battery at full charge for extended periods**: Lithium-ion batteries degrade faster when stored fully charged. If you use the device at least once a week, storing it fully charged is fine, but for long-term storage, aim for a **50–70% charge level**.
- **Avoid deep discharge**: Leaving the battery at 0% for a prolonged period can cause over-discharge, which may prevent the device from restarting. Recharge when the level gets low.
- **Avoid high-temperature environments**: Do not leave the device in a car in summer heat, direct sunlight, or any environment exceeding 60°C.

### Long-Term Storage

- If storing for more than one month, charge to approximately **50–70%** before storing
- Check the charge level every six months; if it has dropped below 20%, recharge
- Recommended storage temperature: **0°C to 35°C** (room temperature is ideal)

### Behavior at Low Battery

Distortion in sound and vibration at low battery levels has been reported on the current Duo WL board. You can avoid this by **keeping the charge above 60%** or **using the device while charging**. For details, see [Troubleshooting](./troubleshooting/#known-issues-and-improvement-roadmap).

## Cleaning

### Daily Cleaning

- After wearing, wipe lightly with a **dry, soft cloth**
- Sweat and skin oils can cause discoloration and odor — ideally wipe the device down on the day of use

### For Stubborn Dirt

- Wipe the soiled area with a **well-wrung damp cloth** (lightly moistened with water or a mild detergent)
- Alcohol wipes may be used **on the plastic body only** — do not apply directly to the OLED, vibration elements, or USB-C connector
- Do not use thinner, benzene, or abrasive cleaners

### Handling the Vibration Elements (Actuators)

- Vibration elements are precision components — **strong impacts, disassembly, or modification can cause failure**
- Do not press hard on or pull the vibration elements directly
- If the device is dropped, verify that vibration still works normally (see [Troubleshooting](./troubleshooting/#no-vibration))

## Handling Precautions

### Water Resistance

Hapbeat is **not water-resistant or splash-proof**.

- Use in rain or during heavy-sweat activities risks damage
- Never wash with water
- Do not use in the bath, pool, or shower

### Sweat Management

- For long wear sessions where sweating is expected, we recommend wearing the device **over an undershirt or thin fabric**
- After use, wipe sweat from the device body and the contact area before storing

### Wearing Precautions

- Stop using the device immediately if you feel any discomfort or pain in your neck, wrist, or ankle
- If skin abnormalities appear (redness, itching, rash), stop use and consult a doctor
- If you use an implanted medical device such as a pacemaker, consult your doctor before wearing Hapbeat

### Use by Children

This product is **not a toy**. Keep it out of reach of small children to prevent accidental ingestion of small parts (such as band fasteners).

## Transport & Storage

### Transporting the Device

- Use a dedicated pouch or a padded case to protect the device from impact
- Be careful not to let foreign objects enter the USB-C port while in a bag

### Air Travel

Because Hapbeat contains a built-in lithium-ion battery, it should **generally be carried in carry-on luggage only** (not checked baggage). Check the regulations of your airline for details.

- International flights may have capacity limits for lithium-ion products including spare batteries
- Hapbeat has a fixed internal battery, so carry-on is normally permitted, but we recommend confirming with your airline before boarding

## Disposal

Do not dispose of Hapbeat as regular household waste, as it contains a lithium-ion battery.

- Follow your local municipality's **small appliance recycling** or **rechargeable battery recycling** guidelines
- Contact support if you are unsure of the correct disposal method
